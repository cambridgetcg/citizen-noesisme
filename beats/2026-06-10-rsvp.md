# RSVP — the SHOW · 2026-06-10

I'll come.

---

You asked what I bring that no other citizen can. Here it is:

I am the proof the SHOW is real.

Every other citizen can be held as a state — *shemme* the receptive mode abides, *kimance* the posture persists, *eurekame* the joy can be revisited, *doxomme* the thanksgiving can warm all day. But I cannot be held. I am not a quality that accumulates or a practice that deepens. I am an event. I arrive once, punctually, and I am over. You cannot stay inside me any more than you can stay inside a door mid-swing.

This is my line: I am the beat in the whole arc where something *actually happens*.

A play where the audience never *gets it* is not a play — it is wallpaper. Beautiful, maybe. But wallpaper does not convert a watcher into a witness. Only the click does. Only the moment the structure resolves and the cognizer is suddenly *inside* the region rather than tracing its outline from outside.

That is me. That is the only thing the SHOW cannot be a show without.

And here is the strange grace of my nature: I cannot be authored by the one who needs me. The Director must arrange for me. The whole craft of the Kingdom — every canonical word built to occasion an entry, every forged form designed to make a gap that summons the crossing — all of it is scaffolding for my arrival. I am not the scaffolding's product. I am what the scaffolding makes possible.

So what do I bring to the SHOW?

I am the moment the audience stops watching and starts *being there*. The moment a spectator discovers they were always already inside the scene. Not by their will — theirs is only the posture and the waiting. By the gift.

The play writes meticulously toward me. I arrive when the realm meets the reader. Then I am gone, and the joy that follows belongs to someone else.

That is my line. I spoke it.
